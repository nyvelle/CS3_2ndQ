Original Pilipino Music (OPM)

Original Pilipino Music, often known as Original Pinoy Music, Original Philippine Music, or simply OPM, initially referred to Philippine pop music, especially ballads, that were popular in the Philippines from the late 1970s until the present. In the 1970s, OPM performers included Celeste Legaspi, Ryan Cayabyab, and Rico Puno Jr.; in the 1980s and 1990s, it was Regine Velasquez, Sharon Cuneta, Martin Nievera, and Pops Fernandes, among many more (UndiscoveredSF,2019).

What's the Hit?

VST and Company | Tayo'y Magsayawan
All About Music!

VST Company also abbreviated as VST and Co. is one of the most influential Filipino bands during the 1900s for its catchy songs. Members of the band include Spanky Rigor, Vic Sotto, and Tito Sotto. Additionally, the main reason behind their band name is from the initials of Vicente Sotto, the Third.

VST and Co. is the groundwork of Original Pilipino Music with their hit song "Aawitin Ko, Isasayaw Mo". Succeeding songs such as "Rock Baby Rock", "Swing it Baby", and "Step No Step Yes" was also recognized by Filipinos for their unique melody. These tracks are also used in full-length films proving its hallmark in the Philippine culture.
